What is it?

Lets Group It is based on the premise that everyone can and wants to improve on their game, but not always have the resources to do so. This is where Lets Group It comes in. We pair up a group of guys with a group of gals on a dine-in restaurant environment where they then can practice their game and simultaneously have fun under "date-like" conditions.

What do you guys do?

Lets Group It is inspired by the popular Japanese tradition of group dating, or also known as, gokon. During a gokon, both parties enjoy a nice dinner, have drinks, and just have a semi-casual, fun social experience. During the gokon, there are leaders in each party, 1 guy and 1 girl. They lead with simple introductions and a single ice breaker. After that, it's all up to you. After the night, we ask you to write a constructive review of your experience for your co-parties so that they could know what their strengths were and what they're weaknesses were.

What does it mean to practice your game and why is it necessary?

Growing up, we take classes to learn about history, math, science, art and have opportunities in the classroom to cultivate those respective skills. But what about developing skills or even courtship? Surely it takes practice just like any other skill! Perhaps in school, you were too busy or too focused to date and missed the opportunity to develop the skills of how to socialize with others and/or develop courtship skills. Well, now here is your chance!

Why it works:

We eliminate the pressure of "being on a blind date" for several reasons:

  1. We don't make it about the blind date because its not even labeled as a date (yet). This is about everyone having a great time, making new friends, making great memories, and preparing yourself socially to achieve your social goals.
  2. You have your best wingmen with you, which eliminates any awkwardness if you were on a date on one.
  3. You don't have to be emotionally invested in this. This is a casual social outing at first. After the Group It, it's all up to you there. There are no expectations.
  4. There is no such thing as rejection, thus eliminating the fear of rejection. Everyone knows that at least one person of each party/group is there to "practice their game."
  5. You have everything to gain: you develop better skills, you have more experience with the opposite sex, you expand your network of friends and your social circles.
